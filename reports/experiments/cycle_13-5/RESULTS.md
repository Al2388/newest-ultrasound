## Interpretation

The ToF-vs-SoC trace shows the **classic LFP staging signature**:

- **0–10 % SoC**: rapid ToF drop (initial intercalation, the "knee")
- **10–70 % SoC**: smooth quasi-linear decline through staged compounds
- **70–100 % SoC**: rebound up to ~−150 ns at full charge

This is non-linear and non-monotonic — a linear fit on the full charge range
under-represents the true sensitivity. A cubic fit captures the shape; the
sigma_ToF reported above is the std of residuals around a smoothed local-median
baseline (so it represents true measurement noise, not deviation from a linear
model).

Temperature is flat within 1.10 °C across the entire 23 h, confirming the C/10
rate produced negligible self-heating.

Cycle-drift check: the first and last cycler-paired A-scans show the echo
packet preserved in shape, shifted forward by ~150 ns consistent with the
predicted ToF change at 100 % SoC. **No additional coupling drift was
detected** beyond what the SoC-driven physics accounts for.

## Known caveat

The peak-to-peak amplitude plot shows saturation at ~3.98 V across most of
the charge cycle — the echo is clipping the CH1 voltage range. The windowed
energy is unaffected and remains the more informative attenuation feature.

**Fix for next run**: increase `ch1_range` from 1.0 V to 2.0 V in the A-scan
config to give the receiver headroom.

## Reproducibility

The full analysis can be regenerated by running:

```
.venv/Scripts/python.exe cycle_analysis.py \
  "data/ascan/ascan_session_2026-05-12_15-42-53/ascan_session_2026-05-12_15-42-53.h5" \
  --cycler "Battery Cycler Data/13-5.xlsx" \
  --temp "pico temperature/13-5.csv" \
  --q-nominal-ah 0.86 \
  --soc-initial-pct 0 \
  --ref-snapshot 0 \
  --out-prefix "13-5 analysis/cycle_13_5"
```

Full stdout from this run is saved in `run_log.txt`.
